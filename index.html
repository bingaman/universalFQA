To upload a new regional FQA database:

1: Log in to your account by clicking the "Log In" at the top of the window. You must be logged in to upload a database.

2: Enter the region, year, and full citation for the database:

Region: (Be brief, but descriptive. For example "Illinois" or "Chicago region")

Year published:

Full citation: (in ... format, provide example)

3: Format the database into a comma-separated file (CSV) spreadsheet file.

The columns should be organized as follows:

				scientific name, common name, acronym, nativity, coefficient of conservatism, coefficient of wetness, physiognomy, duration
Only the scientific name, nativity, and coefficient of conservatism columns are required. The other columns may be left blank.

- scientific name must be standard binomial nomenclature.
- acronym can be a 3-8 letter acronym that is commonly used in field work to abbreviate the scientific name.
- nativity must be either "native" or "non-native". Other terms such as "introduced", "alien", "adventive" will not be recognized.
- coefficient of conservatism must be an integer from 0-10.
- coefficient of wetness can be an integer from 0-10.
- physiognomy can be any of the following terms: "fern", "forb", "grass", "nonvascular" (which includes hornworts, liverworts, and mosses), "sedge", "shrub", "tree", "vine". Other terms won't be recognized.
- duration can be "annual", "biennial", or "perennial". Other terms won't be recognized.

Here are some examples:

				Adiantum pedatum, maidenhair fern, ADIPED, native, 6, 1, fern, perennial (ferns perennials?)****
				Alliaria petiolata, garlic mustard, ALLPET, non-native, 0, 0, forb, biennial

				Alliaria petiolata,,, non-native, 0,,,

About FQA

Floristic Quality Assessment (FQA) is a metric the uses both the aggregate conservatism and species richness of the plant community to derive a measure of habitat condition or quality (Swink and Wilhelm 1979, 1994).
